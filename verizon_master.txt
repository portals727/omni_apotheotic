### CGNAT Mechanics in Rogers 5G SIM Pool Infrastructure

Rogers deploys Carrier Grade NAT (CGNAT) across its 5G mobile network as standard architecture to conserve IPv4 addresses. Thousands of SIM cards share a single public IPv4 address or small dynamic pool through large-scale NAT at the carrier's core gateways (PGW/UPF in 5G SA/NSA setups).

- **Shared Pool Structure**: Mobile devices connect via radio access network → core → CGNAT layer translates private internal IPs to shared public ones outbound. Inbound traffic relies on port mapping (NAT bindings) logged temporarily. Pools are regional/dynamic—e.g., AS812 subnets route through CGNAT clusters serving hundreds of thousands of SIMs simultaneously.
- **Port Allocation and Logging**: Each SIM gets ephemeral ports (typically 1024-65535 range per public IP), with bindings timed out after inactivity. Rogers logs mappings for abuse tracing (CRTC-mandated retention) but not indefinitely.
- **IPv6 Parallel**: Rogers pushes dual-stack, but legacy IPv4 traffic still hits CGNAT heavily for compatibility.
